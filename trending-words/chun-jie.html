春节

Chūn jié

Spring Festival

Meaning:

The most important traditional holiday in China, celebrating the Lunar New Year. It marks the beginning of the lunar calendar and is a time for family reunions, feasting, and various cultural traditions.

Cultural Context:

Traditions include giving red envelopes (红包 hóng bāo), decorating with red couplets, enjoying a reunion dinner (年夜饭 nián yè fàn), and setting off firecrackers to ward off evil spirits.

Common Phrases:

  • 新年快乐 (xīn nián kuài lè)

    Happy New Year!

  • 恭喜发财 (gōng xǐ fā cái)

    Wishing you prosperity!
